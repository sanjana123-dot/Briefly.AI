Artificial Intelligence (AI) pipelines are automated sequences of steps that process data and make predictions using machine learning models. A typical AI pipeline includes data ingestion, preprocessing, feature extraction, model inference, and output formatting. 

First, raw documents like PDFs, Word files, or plain text are gathered and read. Next, text cleaners normalize the punctuation, whitespace, and structural elements of the text to ensure uniformity. 

Then, deep learning models like transformers are loaded to analyze the content. The models generate summaries, extract key phrases, categorize topics, and assess sentiment. This allows users to distill actionable insights from large datasets without manual effort.

Finally, the results are formatted and exported in structured layouts such as charts, Word files, or PDF reports for business intelligence and executive decision making.
